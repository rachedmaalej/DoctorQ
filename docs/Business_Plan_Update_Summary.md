## Risks to Watch

### 1. **Pilot Fails**
**Risk:** What if cousin tries it and it doesn't work?

**Mitigation:**
- Weekly check-ins during pilot (catch issues early)
- Fix bugs within 24 hours (show responsiveness)
- Ask for honest feedback (family = brutal honesty)
- If failing, extend pilot 2 weeks to fix OR pivot product

**Reality:** If it fails with family who wants you to succeed, the product has fundamental issues. Better to learn this early than after 6 months!

### 2. **Cousin's Practice is Atypical**
**Risk:** What if ophthalmology is different from other specialties?

**Mitigation:**
- Interview 15-20 doctors across multiple specialties during customer discovery
- Validate that problem exists for General Medicine, Pediatrics, etc.
- If ophthalmology is unique, adjust ICP to target ophthalmologists first

**Reality:** Wait times are universal across same-day appointment specialties. Ophthalmology is actually ideal (high volume = good test).

### 3. **Cousin Gives Biased Feedback**
**Risk:** What if cousin is too nice and doesn't tell you what's broken?

**Mitigation:**
- Set expectation upfront: "I need BRUTAL honesty. Don't spare my feelings."
- Track objective metrics (wait times, interruptions, patient engagement) - numbers don't lie
- Survey patients independently (they'll be honest even if cousin isn't)
- Watch receptionist behavior (are they actually using it daily?)

**Reality:** Family often gives MORE honest feedback than strangers. Use it.
